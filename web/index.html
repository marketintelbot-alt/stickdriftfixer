How To Use This Tool Correctly

          

            The calibration tool is meant to measure neutral stick behavior, not to permanently repair worn hardware. Start with your controller resting on a flat surface and do not touch either stick during calibration passes. The tool records a sample window and builds deadzone and center values for each axis. If your drift is minor, the profile usually reduces cursor or camera movement at rest. If your drift is severe, software compensation can help temporarily but cannot reverse physical wear in the potentiometer module.

Root Causes of Drift (And What Actually Helps)

          

            Most stick drift comes from wear, dust, oxidation, and spring fatigue inside analog stick assemblies. Firmware updates can improve filtering but cannot fix worn components. Cleaning with compressed air and contact cleaner may reduce symptoms for a limited period. For persistent drift, replacing the analog module is usually the long-term solution.
          

          

            Before replacing parts, verify whether drift appears only on one platform. A controller that drifts on console and PC is usually hardware-limited. A controller that drifts only in one game may need game-specific deadzone tuning.
          

          
    
            
  • Low drift: small neutral movement, often manageable with calibration.
    • 
            
  • Medium drift: intermittent pull; usually needs both cleaning and deadzone tuning.
    • 
            
  • High drift: constant direction pull; often indicates component replacement is due.

Xbox vs PlayStation Button and Input Notes

          

            Input mappings vary by browser and operating system. Xbox controllers usually report ABXY naming directly, while PlayStation controllers may appear with generic button indexes depending on driver translation layers. For this reason, this site reports axis and button behavior by index and gives model-specific notes in the guide. Keep firmware current in Xbox Accessories (Windows/Xbox) or by updating your PlayStation system software and controller firmware.
          

          

            If you use Steam Input, disable duplicate remapping during testing to prevent "double transforms" that make drift appear worse than it is.
